Property Check

Before you offer on a house, get the APN and check the public records. Zillow gives you the listing. This shows you what to verify yourself.

Start here: protect your cash

If your lender wants you to pay credit cards down, ask this first: "How much do I need to pay down, on which specific cards, to hit the score you need?"

Reason: if you only have a limited cash pile, you do not want to drain it all before closing. Pay what the lender says is needed, then keep the rest for closing costs, inspections, appraisal, and moving.

💵

Closing Cost Options

Ask your lender and agent about seller credits, lender credits, CalHFA assistance, and FHA gift funds. Do not guess. Have them put numbers on paper.

🧾

Seller Credits

FHA rules allow interested-party contributions within program limits. Your agent writes seller credit terms into the offer when it makes sense.

🏛️

CalHFA MyHome

CalHFA describes MyHome as assistance for down payment and/or closing costs. Terms change, so verify the current program page before planning around it.

Step 1: Get the APN

The APN is the Assessor Parcel Number. It is the key that unlocks the public records for that property.

  1. Check the official owner

    Use Riverside County Assessor records to compare the listing against the owner of record. If the owner is an LLC, trust, or estate, that changes what questions you ask before offering.

    Open Property Search

  2. Check permits

    Use Murrieta's permit portal for additions, remodels, water heaters, HVAC, solar, patio covers, and other work. If the house changed but the permit record does not match, ask your agent before moving forward.

    Open Permit Search

  3. Compare square footage

    Compare the listing square footage to the assessor record. If the listing says more than the county record, ask why. It could be a mistake, an addition, or something that was not permitted.

    Check Assessor Record

  4. Pull the full tax bill

    Use the Riverside County tax bill by APN. Look for special assessments and Mello-Roos. Do not trust a generic payment estimate until you check the real tax bill.

    Open Tax Search

  5. Estimate your reassessed tax

    In California, a purchase can create a new assessed value. That means the current owner's tax bill may be lower than yours will be. Budget off your purchase price, not the seller's old tax basis.

    Read CA BOE Rule

The offer question

After you check the APN, ask your agent this exact question: "Based on the owner, permits, tax bill, and Mello-Roos, is there anything here that should change my offer?"

That is the point of this page. Not to scare you. To keep you from finding out expensive stuff after you are already emotionally attached.
