Glossary — Real Estate Words in Plain English

The words you'll hear, explained the way a friend would explain them.

FHA Loan
A home loan backed by the government (Federal Housing Administration). It's popular with first-time buyers because the down payment can be as low as 3.5% and the credit score requirements are easier than regular loans.
Down Payment
The cash you put in upfront when you buy. If a house costs $500,000 and you put 3.5% down, that's $17,500 from your pocket, and the loan covers the rest.
Pre-Approval
A letter from a lender saying "we checked this person's finances and we'll lend them up to $X." It tells sellers you're a serious buyer.
Escrow
A neutral third party that holds the money and paperwork during the sale so nobody can cheat anybody. "Being in escrow" means the deal is in progress. After you own the home, "escrow account" also means the little savings account your lender keeps to pay your taxes and insurance.
Closing Costs
The fees to finish the deal — lender fees, escrow fees, title insurance, appraisal, and more. Usually 2–5% of the home price, paid on top of your down payment.
DTI (Debt-to-Income Ratio)
How much of your monthly income already goes to debt payments. If you earn $6,000 a month and pay $2,400 toward debts (including the new house payment), your DTI is 40%. Lenders use this to decide how much to loan you.
PMI / MIP (Mortgage Insurance)
An extra monthly fee that protects the lender (not you) if you stop paying. You usually pay it when your down payment is under 20%. On FHA loans it's called MIP and it's required.
Interest Rate vs. APR
The interest rate is what the loan itself costs per year. APR is the rate plus the lender's fees rolled in, so APR is the better number for comparing lenders.
Appraisal
A professional's official opinion of what the house is worth. The lender requires it so they don't loan more than the house's value.
Home Inspection
A professional checks the house for problems — roof, plumbing, electrical, foundation. You pay for this, and it's worth every penny.
Contingency
An "escape hatch" written into your offer. Example: an inspection contingency means if the inspection finds something terrible, you can walk away and keep your deposit.
Earnest Money
A deposit (often 1–3% of the price) you put down with your offer to show you're serious. It goes toward your down payment if the deal closes.
Title
The legal record of who owns the home. Title insurance protects you if someone later claims they have a right to your property.
HOA (Homeowners Association)
In many Murrieta and Temecula neighborhoods, an organization that maintains shared areas and enforces rules. You pay a monthly fee, so always ask if a home has an HOA and how much it costs.
CalHFA
The California Housing Finance Agency — a state agency with programs that help first-time buyers with down payments and closing costs. Worth checking at calhfa.ca.gov.
Fixed vs. Adjustable Rate
Fixed rate: your payment stays the same for the whole loan (most first-time buyers choose this). Adjustable (ARM): starts lower but can go up later.
