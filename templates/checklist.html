First-Time Buyer Checklist

Do these in order. One step at a time. You don't have to rush.

  1. Check your credit score

    Lenders look at your credit score first. For an FHA loan you generally need at least 580 for the lowest down payment (3.5%). Get your free reports at AnnualCreditReport.com and fix any errors before you apply.

  2. Figure out what you can afford

    A rough rule: your total monthly house payment (loan + taxes + insurance) should stay under about a third of your monthly income before taxes. Don't forget Riverside County property taxes and possible HOA fees.

  3. Save for the down payment and closing costs

    FHA down payment is as low as 3.5% of the price. Closing costs are usually another 2–5%. Look into CalHFA down payment assistance — California has programs that can cover some of this for first-time buyers.

  4. Get pre-approved by a lender

    Pre-approval is a letter from a lender saying how much they'll loan you. Sellers take you seriously with one. Shop at least 2–3 lenders and compare their rates and fees — they are not all the same.

  5. Find a real estate agent

    Pick an agent who works in Murrieta/Temecula and knows first-time buyers. The agent's commission is usually paid out of the sale, so it typically costs you nothing out of pocket to have one on your side.

  6. Shop for homes and make an offer

    Tour homes in your budget. When you find one, your agent helps you write an offer. The seller can accept, reject, or counter. This part can take a few tries — that's normal.

  7. Open escrow

    When the seller accepts, a neutral company (escrow) holds the money and paperwork while everything gets checked. In California this usually takes 30–45 days.

  8. Get the home inspected and appraised

    You pay an inspector (a few hundred dollars) to find problems with the house. The lender orders an appraisal to confirm the house is worth the price. If the inspection finds big issues, you can ask the seller to fix them or lower the price.

  9. Final walkthrough and signing

    Right before closing, you walk through the house one last time to make sure it's in the agreed condition. Then you sign the final loan papers (a lot of them) with a notary.

  10. Close and get your keys 🎉

    Your loan funds, the county records the sale, and the house is yours. You get the keys, and your first mortgage payment usually isn't due until a month or more after closing.
